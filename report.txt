# Student Information Report: Application Knowledge Overview

This report outlines the categories and specific pieces of information that the Shinchronize application collects, maintains, and derives about its users (students).

---

## 1. Identity & Personal Profile
The application maintains a comprehensive personal profile for each student:
*   **Basic Identity:** Full name (First & Last), Email address (used for login and communication), and Gender.
*   **Contact Information:** Phone number and current Location (City/Country).
*   **Profile Visuals:** Avatar/Profile Picture.
*   **Bio/Professional Narrative:** A professional headline and a long-form "About You" summary.

## 2. Academic Background
To help recruiters filter by academic criteria, the app tracks:
*   **Institution:** College name.
*   **Specialization:** Department or Branch of study.
*   **Progress:** Current year of study and expected Graduation Year.
*   **Performance:** CGPA/GPA (on a 10-point scale).

## 3. Professional Presence & Online Identity
The app serves as a hub for the student's various professional links:
*   **Social & Professional:** LinkedIn profile, GitHub profile.
*   **Work Samples:** Portfolio/Personal website URL and Resume link.
*   **Coding Platform Handles:** LeetCode and HackerRank usernames.

## 4. Skills & Technical Expertise
The application builds a technical profile through:
*   **Self-Reported Skills:** A list of technical and soft skills (e.g., JavaScript, React, System Design).
*   **Skill Radar Data:** A mapping of specific skill categories to numeric scores, often visualized in a radar chart.
*   **LinkedIn Integration:** The ability to auto-populate skills, headline, and summary by parsing an uploaded LinkedIn PDF export.

## 5. Tangible Evidence of Work
The platform acts as a repository for proof-of-work:
*   **Projects:** Comprehensive details including title, description, GitHub links, and tags.
*   **Verification Status:** Projects go through a lifecycle (Pending â†’ Verified/Rejected). The app knows which admin verified a project and any feedback they provided.
*   **Certifications:** Records of professional certifications, including the provider, issue date, and certificate ID.
*   **Events:** Participation in hackathons or events, including outcomes and points awarded.

## 6. Daily Activity & Performance Tracking
Shinchronize tracks habits and consistency:
*   **Coding Logs:** Detailed logs of practice sessions (date, platform, activity type, minutes spent, and problems solved).
*   **Streaks:** Consecutive days of activity on the platform.
*   **Engagement:** "Last active" timestamps and profile completion status.

## 7. Deep Analytics (External Integration)
Specifically for LeetCode, the application performs a deep sync to understand external performance:
*   **Problem-Solving Stats:** Total problems solved, broken down by difficulty (Easy, Medium, Hard).
*   **Domain Strengths:** Identification of top problem-solving domains (e.g., Arrays, Dynamic Programming).
*   **Historical Activity:** A 52-week submission calendar (heatmap) and weekly submission trends.
*   **Best Performance:** Data on the student's highest performance days (most submissions in 24 hours).

## 8. Placement Readiness & Intelligence
The application uses the collected data to derive strategic insights:
*   **Readiness Score:** An overall percentage indicating how "ready" a student is for placement.
*   **Score Breakdown:** Analysis of how the score is composed (Consistency, Growth Rate, Skill Diversity, Projects).
*   **Predictive Insights:** Projected timelines to reach benchmarks and milestones.
*   **AI Growth Story:** An AI-generated narrative that summarizes the student's journey, highlighting resilience, adaptability, and specific strengths.

## 9. Professional Interest
The application keeps track of "Market Interest":
*   **Recruiter Interest:** It knows which recruiters have "saved" the student for later consideration.
